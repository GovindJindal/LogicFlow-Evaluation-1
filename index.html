Overview

What is LogicFlow?

LogicFlow is a browser-based electronics and digital-logic learning environment. Run experiments, build intuition with interactive instruments, and follow curriculum-aligned labs—without waiting for a physical bench or risking damaged parts.

◎

Purpose-built for learning

Every screen is designed for clarity: schematics, meters, and graphs update in real time as you change bias, frequency, or logic inputs.

◇

Always available

Open the lab from any modern browser. Practice at your pace, revisit tricky concepts, and prepare for assessments with repeatable experiments.

What you get

Labs, tools, and structure

  • Hands-on experiments — From components and bench equipment to diodes, rectifiers, flip-flops, and decoders.
  • Logic gate sandbox — Compose and reason about digital building blocks (roadmap; some areas marked in development).
  • Computer architecture visuals — Step through pipeline-style activities to connect hardware concepts to systems thinking.
  • Curriculum mapping — See how experiments align with course outcomes and paper codes where institutions provide mappings.
  • Export-friendly workflows — Structured lab flows that support notes and submissions where enabled.

What’s innovative

Why LogicFlow feels different

Interactive fidelity

Controls behave like instruments: ranges, limits, and visual feedback mirror how you’d probe a real circuit—without the fragility of hardware-only labs.

Unified narrative

Analog electronics, digital logic, and architecture concepts live in one coherent journey instead of scattered PDFs and simulators.

Institution-ready (optional)

Universities can pair registered access with class rosters; independent learners keep full open access. The product is built to support both models.

How it works

From landing to lab in minutes

  1. 1 Explore the site — Use the overview sections to understand what LogicFlow offers.
  2. 2 Enter the lab — Choose student, faculty, or guest access. Guests and open learners use the full experience without institution login.
  3. 3 Pick a path — Follow curriculum mapping, dive into experiments, or open specialized tools like the gate sandbox or architecture activities.
  4. 4 Save progress (when enrolled) — With institutional sign-in, activity can sync for instructors; guest mode stays private and untracked.

Who built it

Built for the classroom—and beyond

LogicFlow is developed as a front-end engineering and learning-design project focused on accessible, high-quality STEM practice. It combines careful UI work, curriculum alignment, and interactive simulations so students can learn by doing, wherever they are.

This deployment is a frontend demonstration. Backend authentication, gradebooks, and analytics would connect here in a production rollout.
